AHMED FARAZ

Syed Ahmad Shah, better known by his pen name Ahmed Faraz, was a Pakistani Urdu poet, scriptwriter and became the founding director general of Pakistan Academy of letters. Faraz was born on 12 january 1931 in Kohat. He belonged to a Pashtun Syed family. Faraz is credited for writing "Pas Andaaz", "Sab Awazein Meri", "Khuwab Gul", "Janan Janan", and "Ghazal Bahana Karoon". Ahmed faraz served as chairman of the National Book Foundation in Islamabad, Pakistan. Faraz was arrested for writing poems that criticised military rulers in Pakistan during the reign of General Zia-ul-Haq. Ahmed Faraz was first awarded the "Sitara-i-Imtiaz" by the Government of Pakistan and then the "Hilal-i-Imtiaz" in 2004 by the then President of Pakistan Pervez Musharraf. He returned this award two years laer in 2006.On 25 auagust 2008, he died in Islamabad, and later Government of Pakistan conferred "Hilal-e-Pakistan upon faraz for his contribution to poetry and Urdu literature.
